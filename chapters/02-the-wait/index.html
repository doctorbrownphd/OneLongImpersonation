Chapter 02

The Wait.

Have you heard how long Judas Priest waited? They became eligible in 1999. Twenty-three years later, they still haven't been inducted as Performers. Iron Maiden waited 21 years. Ozzy waited 29. The clock runs differently depending on what you sound like.

Ex. 002-C -- Cox Proportional Hazards Model

A survival model estimates each covariate's effect on the annual probability of induction. A hazard ratio below 1.0 means slower induction; above 1.0 means faster.

Causation caveat

This model documents disparities in induction rates. It does not prove intentional discrimination. Observational data, correlation only.

Methodology

What this chapter does: We model time-to-induction using a Cox Proportional Hazards model fitted to 256 inducted Performer-category artists. Each artist's wait time is measured from first eligibility (25 years after first commercial recording) to induction year. Covariates include race, sex, solo/group status, primary genre, and eligibility era (pre- vs. post-2000).

Survival curves: Kaplan-Meier estimates are shown for four strata: White male Classic Rock artists (the baseline), Black Soul/R&B artists, Female artists, and Heavy Metal artists. These are empirical survival functions, not model predictions.

Data sources: Rock and Roll Hall of Fame official inductee database. Eligibility year computed from MusicBrainz first-release dates. Race, sex, and genre classified from primary biographical sources.

What this chapter cannot show: The Cox model documents disparities. A hazard ratio of 0.66 for female artists means they are inducted roughly 34% slower per year than the baseline group, controlling for other covariates. This does not prove intentional bias. The reader draws conclusions.
