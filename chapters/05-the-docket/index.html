Ex. 005 -- The Docket

AI-Generated Content Disclosure

Individual artist briefs in The Docket are generated by Claude (claude-sonnet-4-20250514). Each brief is generated from a structured prompt containing only documented facts from the pipeline database, not from the model's training data.

Verdict classifications (Strong Case, Case, Borderline, Insufficient) are computed from Criteria Compliance Score percentile thresholds, not from Claude's judgment.

All generated briefs are reviewed by a human editor before publication. Factual errors are corrected. Unsourced claims are removed. The review process is documented in each brief's metadata.

Methodology

What this chapter does: Every eligible artist who has not been inducted receives a structured brief applying the Hall's five stated criteria to their documented record. The brief is generated by Claude API from a structured prompt containing only facts from our pipeline database.

Verdict classification: Computed from the artist's Criteria Compliance Score relative to inducted artists. STRONG CASE = score above 75th percentile of inducted artists. CASE = above 50th. BORDERLINE = above 25th. INSUFFICIENT = below 25th.

What this chapter cannot show: The Docket does not recommend who should be inducted. It applies the Hall's own stated criteria to documented facts and reports the result. The verdict is computed, not advocated.

Annual update: The Docket is updated annually in January, before the Hall announces nominations. Updated after nominations to show which cases were addressed.
