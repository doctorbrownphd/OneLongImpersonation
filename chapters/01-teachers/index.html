Have you heard of Sister Rosetta Tharpe? She invented the electric guitar style that Chuck Berry built his career on. Berry was inducted in 1986, the first ceremony. He acknowledged her influence throughout his career. As he reportedly said: "My whole career has been one long Sister Rosetta Tharpe impersonation." Tharpe was inducted in 2018. Methodology

What this chapter does: For every inducted artist, we identify documented teacher-student influence relationships from primary sources. We then measure the gap between when the student was inducted and when the teacher was inducted (or whether the teacher was ever inducted at all). The distribution of this gap, broken down by the racial composition of each pair, is the chapter's central finding.

Data sources: Curated influence pairs verified from interviews, liner notes, published biographies, and MusicBrainz artist relationship data. Every pair is individually sourced. See pipeline/data/curated/influence_pairs.yaml.

Confidence level: All featured pairs are labeled "Documented" -- sourced to primary evidence. The median gap statistics are computed from this documented dataset only. As the MusicBrainz-sourced influence graph grows, these numbers may shift. We will document any changes in the version history.

What this chapter cannot show: The gap metric documents a disparity in induction timing. It does not prove that the disparity was intentional. The racial breakdown is calculated, not asserted. The reader draws conclusions.
