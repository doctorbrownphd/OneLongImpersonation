The Criteria.

Before the prosecution begins, the jury must know the law. The Rock and Roll Hall of Fame publishes five criteria for induction. This chapter presents them, unedited, alongside the measures this report uses to test whether they were followed.

Three Axes of Prosecution

Pioneers

Black artists, women, and genre inventors who built the music the Hall celebrates. How long did the teachers wait while the students walked in first?

Genres

Entire musical traditions excluded or systematically underrepresented. Which genres got a seat at the table and which were left outside?

Voters

The nominating committee, documented. Who decides what counts as rock and roll, and what patterns emerge from their choices?

The Hall's Own Criteria

What they said they would do.

The Rock and Roll Hall of Fame publishes five criteria for induction. Every claim in this report is measured against them. Below is each criterion, paired with the proxy measures we use to operationalize it.

Criterion Proxy Measures
Musical Excellence Grammy wins and nominations, critical consensus scores, peer recognition (tributes, cover recordings, sampled works).
Influence on Other Performers Teacher-student links documented in liner notes, interviews, and biographies. Induction-gap delta between influencer and influenced.
Length of Career Years from first commercial release to present. Eligibility wait time (years between eligibility and induction).
Depth of Catalog Studio album count, charted singles, catalog persistence (streams, reissues, sync licenses).
Contributions to Rock and Roll Genre origination or transformation documented by musicologists. Cross-genre pollination. Innovation in production, performance, or form.

Now you know what they said they would do. The next chapter is the first thing they did instead.
